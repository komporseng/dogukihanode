Jose Altuve Baseball Career

José Altuve’s journey in the world of baseball began when he signed as an undrafted free agent with the Houston Astros on March 6, 2007. His talent quickly shone through, and it only took him four years to be promoted to the major leagues. In 2011, José ended his first season with a batting average of .276, and he has consistently been a force to reckon with ever since.

He had a remarkable 2014 season, becoming the first MLB player since 1933 to achieve 130 hits and 40 stolen bases before the All-Star Break. That year, he was named to the 2014 All-Star Game and received numerous awards, including the GIBBY/This Year in Baseball Award and the Breakout Everyday Player of the Year.

José continued to rise to fame, recording his 800th career hit in September 2015 and ending the season with 38 stolen bases and a .313 batting average. In 2015, he earned his first Rawlings Gold Glove Award and his first Silver Slugger Award. His outstanding performance in June 2016 earned him the AL Player of the Month title, with an impressive .420 batting average, six doubles, four home runs, 15 RBIs, and six stolen bases in just 26 games. In 2016, he achieved another milestone by hitting his 1000th career hit.

His outstanding performances continued in 2017, helping the Astros secure the World Series Championship. José Altuve received numerous awards that year, including the Silver Slugger Award and the Hank Aaron Award. In 2019, he marked his 100th career home run and his 1500th career hit. On September 17, 2021, he tied the record for the most hits by an Astro in Minute Maid Park, and the next night, he broke that record with a double.
