What is Stevie Wonder’s Net Worth?

Stevie Wonder, the American musician, singer, songwriter, and record producer, has a net worth of $200 million dollars. He is considered a genius due to his multi-instrumentalism, composition, and songwriting abilities. Wonder signed with Motown Records at 11 years old and has had 30 #1 hits, including “Superstition,” “Isn’t She Lovely,” and “I Just Called to Say I Love You.”

Early Life and Career

Stevland Hardaway Judkins was born in Saginaw, Michigan on May 13, 1950. He is the third of six children of songwriter Lula Mae Hardaway and Calvin Judkins. Wonder was born six weeks premature, which led to his blindness due to a condition known as retinopathy of prematurity. His parents divorced when he was four, and he moved to Detroit with his mother and siblings. As a child, he sang in the choir of his local Baptist Church and showed musical talent by easily picking up the piano, drums, and harmonica.

Wonder’s blindness heightened his awareness of sound and allowed him to create vibrant and colorful music. His talent was soon recognized by Motown CEO Berry Gordy, and in 1961, at the age of 11, he signed with Motown.

Stevie Wonder’s Life and Career

Stevie Wonder signed a five-year contract with Motown at the age of 11, with royalties placed in a trust until he turned 21. He released his first album, “Tribute to Uncle Ray,” at age 11, followed by “The Jazz Soul of Little Stevie.” Wonder joined the Motortown Revue in 1962 and released “Recorded Live: The 12-Year-Old Genius” in 1963, with the hit single “Fingertips.” He scored several hits between 1968 and 1970 and married Syreeta Wright in 1970, with whom he worked on the album “Where I’m Coming From.”

Wonder’s Motown contract expired when he turned 21, and he signed a new contract with a much higher royalty rate. He returned to Motown in 1972 with “Music of My Mind” and released “Talking Book” in late 1972, featuring the number one hits “Superstition” and “You Are the Sunshine of My Life.” His album “Innervisions” won Album of the Year and is ranked No. 23 on Rolling Stone’s 500 Greatest Albums of All Time. Wonder’s best-known works include “Superstition,” “Sir Duke,” and “Songs in the Key of Life.” He has received 25 Grammy Awards and is also appreciated for his political activism, including his campaign to make Martin Luther King Jr.’s birthday a holiday in the United States. Wonder was inducted into The Soul Music Hall of Fame in December 2012.

Stevie Wonder’s Marriages and Children

Stevie Wonder has been married three times and has nine children by five different women. He married Syreeta Wright in 1970, and they divorced amicably in 1972. He was married to Kai Millard from 2001 to 2012. In 2017, he married Tomeeka Bracy.

Wonder’s oldest child is Aisha Morris, born in 1975 to Yolanda Simmons. She is a singer who has toured with her father. They also had a son, Keita, in 1977. In 1983, Wonder had a son, Mumtaz Morris, with Melody McCully. He has a daughter, Sophia, and a son, Kwame, with an undisclosed woman. He has two sons, Kialand and Mandla, with Kai Millard. His ninth child, Nia, was born in 2014 to Tomeeka Bracy. The name of his first child with Bracy is unknown.

Stevie Wonder’s Real Estate Transactions

In 2009, Stevie Wonder purchased a 4,511 sq ft Mediterranean estate in Los Angeles’ Los Feliz neighborhood for $2.407 million. Four years later, he listed the property for sale at $8.199 million.

In December 2021, Stevie Wonder purchased a 20,000 sq ft mansion in Bel Air for $14 million. The seller was a member of the Saudi Royal Family.
